Bulbul Hasan

YOUR WORLDWIDE MONTESSORI MENTOR

I come from a family of educators – both my parents worked as teachers at the high school and university level and I was always attracted to the idea of being a teacher from the time I was little.

While I was finishing a Bachelor’s degree in Psychology, Literature and Journalism in Bangalore India, I was introduced to a Krishnamurthi school community on the outskirts of the city where I ended up for a year in their Post school Program where I was first introduced to the idea of education that looked at the ‘whole child’ and as opposed to the regular, traditional form of education with a clear focus on external results, explored alternative ways of working with children that were slightly more process oriented.

I ended up assisting a teacher who had worked closely with the Rudolf Steiner philosophy and I began to enjoy her approach to and relationship with the children and the varied activities she carried out with them in different settings – nature walks and art work were some of my favourites and living and contributing to the large school community for a year at CFL made me understand the beginnings of the impact education can have on society and vice versa. https://cfl.in/j-krishnamurti/

I then went on to work with a group of 8 year olds at a school that followed the International Baccalaureate Program and trained to offer them a curriculum based on the Primary Years Programme. https://indusschool.com/

Another significant experience for me was returning to work with younger children at a Reggio Emilio inspired Pre School for 2- 7 year olds. When assigned the task of creating a curriculum for this age group, I began to feel the need to understand this age group, more deeply, in relation to their developmental needs and upon the suggestion of an avid Montessorian and close friend, I decided to take on a Montessori Diploma Course for 3-6 year olds at Navadisha Montessori Institute in Chennai, India. https://navadisha.org/

Looking back, pursuing this 3-6 AMI training in Chennai was significant in my journey as an educator because it set me on a focused path of a discovery of children this age group and the skills required to create educational environments that helped them achieve the utmost potential of their growing years.

Also, little did I know that completing this course would shape my career and enable me to travel and gain valuable work and life experience in varied Montessori schools across the globe.

While my AMI training was a perfect blend of theory and hours of practicing with the materials designed for this age group, working with children and parents in different schools around the world gave me first hand experience that went on to form the basis of the work that I continue to carry out with children and adults. I enjoy backing my lectures and planning my workshops, keeping in mind the fodder of real life experiences, anecdotes and examples in mind and I often use photos that I have collected over the years to exemplify what I consider my deepest passion – implementing and sharing the Montessori philosophy in theory and practice around the world.
